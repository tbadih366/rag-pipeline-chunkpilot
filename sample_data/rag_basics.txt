Retrieval-Augmented Generation, or RAG, is a pattern where we search trusted documents first and then use those results to answer a question.
Without retrieval, a language model may guess from memory and produce hallucinations.
RAG improves groundedness by attaching evidence from source documents.
The standard pipeline is ingest documents, chunk text, embed chunks, store vectors, retrieve relevant chunks, and then generate an answer.
In production systems, evaluation often tracks retrieval precision, answer faithfulness, latency, and cost.
